Forty degrees is the sweet spot where most runners make clothing mistakes. Too many layers and you're overheating by mile two. Too few and the first half-mile is miserable. Here's how to get it right.

## The Runner's +15°F Rule

Your body generates significant heat while running — roughly 15–20°F worth of perceived warmth above what a sedentary person feels. This means at 40°F, you should dress as if it's **55°F**. That's the "dress for 15–20 degrees warmer" rule that experienced runners swear by.

Should I Run takes this further by adjusting for your **effort level**:

| Effort | Heat Offset | Dress-For Temp at 40°F |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Easy | +15°F | 55°F |
| Endurance | +17°F | 57°F |
| Tempo | +20°F | 60°F |
| Threshold | +22°F | 62°F |
| Interval | +25°F | 65°F |

An easy 40°F run and a tempo 40°F run require completely different clothing.

## What to Wear: The 40°F Tier

For an **easy run** at 40°F (dressing for ~55°F):

**Bottom:** Shorts or half tights — you may be surprised, but most acclimated runners are fine in shorts at this temperature. Alternatives: capris or joggers if you prefer coverage.

**Top:** Long-sleeve tech tee. A lightweight half-zip or thin hoodie also works well. The key is a single breathable layer that wicks moisture.

**Accessories:** Lightweight gloves (you'll likely pocket these by mile 2) and a headband or ear cover. Your extremities cool fastest, but your core will be generating plenty of heat.

## Wind Changes Everything

Wind is the hidden variable at 40°F. The NWS wind chill formula tells us:

- 40°F + 10 mph wind = feels like 34°F
- 40°F + 20 mph wind = feels like 30°F

If wind chill drops you into the 30s, bump up one clothing tier: running tights instead of shorts, add a half-zip over your long sleeve, and upgrade to warmer gloves.

## The "Runs Hot" Factor

Some runners generate more metabolic heat than others. If you're someone who's always shedding layers, Should I Run's "Runs hot" toggle adds an extra +5°F offset to your clothing calculation. At 40°F, that means dressing for 60°F instead of 55°F — likely shorts and a t-shirt territory for easy runs.

## Common Mistakes at 40°F

1. **Wearing a heavy jacket.** You'll overheat within 10 minutes. A breathable long sleeve is almost always enough at this temperature.
2. **Skipping gloves.** Your hands will be cold for the first mile. Lightweight gloves are worth it even if you stash them later.
3. **Overdressing the bottom half.** Your legs have large muscle groups generating heat. Shorts work for most runners down to the mid-30s.

## Bottom Line

Forty degrees is nearly ideal running weather — you just need the right layers. Start with a long-sleeve tech tee, shorts or half tights, and light gloves. Adjust up for wind, down for harder efforts. And let [Should I Run](/) do the math for you based on today's actual conditions.
