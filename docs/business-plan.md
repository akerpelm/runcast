# RunCast — Business Plan v2

*Your morning running briefing: not just what to wear, but what to do.*

---

## What Exists Today

DressMyRun.com is a solid free tool. Looking at the actual product (Brooklyn, March 15, 2026):

- Hourly temperature timeline with weather icons and UV index
- Location picker, date picker, hour scrubber — all free, unlimited
- "Wear this for your run" section: Gloves, Long Sleeve Shirt, Jacket, Tights
- Temperature preference toggle (Normal / Runs Hot / Runs Cold)
- Powered by Apple Weather

It's clean. It works. And it's 100% free with no apparent monetization.

**What it doesn't do:** Tell you anything about how to *run*. It's a wardrobe assistant, not a running assistant.

---

## The Idea

**DressMyRun tells you what to wear. RunCast tells you how to run today.**

Same weather data as the foundation, but layered with actionable running intelligence.

### The Free Core (Must Be Better Than DressMyRun)

1. **Pace adjustment for conditions** — temp + dew point method, user enters base easy pace once, RunCast adjusts daily
2. **Surface & safety warnings** — ice on sidewalks, muddy trails, wind gusts
3. **Run type suggestion** — easy day vs tempo vs treadmill based on conditions
4. **Best window picker** — scans hourly forecast for optimal running window
5. **AQI + pollen overlay** — simple traffic light
6. **Sunrise/sunset with run math** — daylight remaining at adjusted pace
7. **Clothing recs** — match DressMyRun parity

### The Feedback Loop (Free, Optional, Data Collection)

- Post-run 1-tap feedback: too hot / just right / too cold + pace feel
- Aggregate dataset is the long-term moat

---

## Monetization: No Subscription

1. **Contextual affiliate gear links** (primary) — "Shop picks" row under clothing recs
2. **Seasonal content + SEO** — targeting "what to wear running in X" queries
3. **Brand partnerships** (month 6+) — sponsored gear slots, data insights
